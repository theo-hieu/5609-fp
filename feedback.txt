This project really stood out in the previous milestone, though it feels a bit less distinctive now as other groups are making strong progress.

Think about how to move beyond showing three separate line charts. Right now, the first three charts feel somewhat repetitive, and the current animation of the lines simply growing is a bit basic. It may be more effective to use animation to show the relationships among the three line chart. For example, the increase in average shot distance is coming from taking more 3-point shots and fewer 2-point shots (2nd line chart), which can be further explained that above-the-break 3s increase a lot, while left- and right-corner 3s stay fairly similar. Highlighting these transitions could make the story much clearer.

You could also consider using more meaningful color encoding for the lines based on distance. For example, left-corner 3 could use a darker tone than shots closer to the restricted area.

For the double y-axis chart, instead of placing two axes in the same figure, you might try a stacked bar chart for the histogram, with made and missed shots stacked together. Then animation could help transition between showing percentages on the y-axis and showing number of attempts.

Since the final project is expected to go beyond standard visualizations, keep thinking about how to address that more clearly.

It may also help to explore a multi-view design that better connects the currently separate views (line, heatmap). Right now, users may need more guidance on how to read and compare the heatmap visualizations, e.g., which year to choose and how to compare across years. Looking back at the class examples may give you some ideas
